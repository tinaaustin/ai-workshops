# Course AI Policy Starter

A lightweight starting point for educators writing AI guidance for a course or assignment.

This is not legal advice or a complete institutional policy. It is a public orientation resource. The full UnBlooms book includes more detailed implementation support.

## Short Version

In this course, AI tools may be used only when they support the learning goals of the assignment. You are responsible for understanding, verifying, and explaining any work you submit.

## What Students Should Know

- AI use must follow the specific instructions for each assignment.
- You may be asked to explain how you completed your work.
- You are responsible for checking accuracy, sources, and reasoning.
- AI-generated text, citations, data, or code may contain errors.
- When AI is used, you may need to disclose how it supported your process.

## Assignment-Level Options

### Option A: AI Not Allowed

AI tools may not be used for this assignment. The purpose of the task is to practice unaided human thinking, performance, memory, judgment, or skill.

### Option B: AI Allowed For Support

AI may be used for support such as brainstorming, outlining, revising, or generating study questions. The final work must reflect your own understanding and judgment.

### Option C: AI Required For Critique

You will use AI output as material to evaluate. Your grade will depend on how well you identify strengths, errors, gaps, assumptions, and needed revisions.

### Option D: AI Allowed With Documentation

AI may be used, but you must include a brief transparency statement explaining what tool you used, what it helped with, what you changed, and what you verified.
