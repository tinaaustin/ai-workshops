# Faculty AI Conversation Starters

Short prompts for faculty meetings, professional development sessions, and course redesign conversations about AI.

These are meant to open discussion, not replace the full UnBlooms workbook or implementation materials.

## Teaching And Learning

- What kinds of thinking do we most want students to practice in this course?
- Which assignments still work if students use AI?
- Which assignments only appear to work because we cannot see the process?
- Where do students need more guidance, not more policing?

## Assessment

- What evidence of learning do we currently collect?
- What evidence of process could we reasonably ask students to show?
- Which assessments should be AI-constrained?
- Which assessments could become stronger if students critique AI output?

## Student Support

- What do students already misunderstand about AI?
- Where might AI help students access a task?
- Where might AI quietly prevent students from building skill?
- How do we explain acceptable use clearly and consistently?

## Equity And Access

- Do all students have comparable access to approved tools?
- Are we assuming students know how to use AI well?
- Could AI policies create uneven advantages?
- What support do students need to use AI critically?

## Program-Level Questions

- What should students in our program know about AI by graduation?
- Where should AI literacy be introduced, practiced, and assessed?
- Which disciplines or courses need different boundaries?
- What shared language would help faculty and students?
